Ridit Uppal
Ever since I first encountered mandala art, it felt like I had stumbled upon something that spoke directly to my soul. There’s something deeply captivating about the way these intricate, geometric patterns seem to come to life, drawing you in with their symmetry and rhythm. It’s more than just a visual experience—it’s a spiritual one, too. Each line, curve, and dot feels like a thread in a greater tapestry, and when I create a mandala, I feel like I’m part of something much larger than myself. I’m drawn to the repetition in mandalas, the process of carefully layering shapes, patterns, and colors. The more I work on them, the more I lose track of time, entering a meditative state. It’s almost like the world fades away, and I’m left with only the art and my thoughts. It’s a deeply grounding practice—an escape that brings peace and clarity, no matter how chaotic life might feel. What excites me most is the endless possibility within the structure of a mandala. There’s a balance of order and freedom; while the form is strict, the creativity within it is limitless. Every design I create feels like a new journey, a new discovery. Sometimes I’ll start with a simple idea, a basic shape, and as I go along, the mandala evolves into something I never could have predicted. I love how every piece is unique, a reflection of where I am in that moment. And then there’s the healing aspect. Mandalas are often associated with spiritual growth, balance, and mindfulness, and I’ve experienced this firsthand. The process of creating one brings me a sense of inner peace and wholeness. I feel a connection to something universal, like my heart is aligning with the rhythm of the universe itself. To me, mandala art is more than just decoration—it’s a language that speaks to my deepest emotions and my highest self. It’s a practice of self-expression and self-discovery, and I’m constantly amazed by how much there is to explore within this beautiful, circular world.
